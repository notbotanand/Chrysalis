## Business Overview
Globex Wallet is a Series B fintech building a unified wallet experience
for small-business owners — combining banking, payments, and tax-aware
expense tracking. Founded 2021, raised $35M Series B in late 2024.
~85 employees, HQ in Brooklyn NY with remote engineering across US/EU.
([Globex Series B announcement](https://example.com/globex-series-b))

broad launch H2 2026. Revenue not disclosed; signals suggest pre-revenue

podcasts. The user base skews younger (under 40) and digital-native —
self-onboarded, expects mobile-first UX.

## Profile-Aware Analysis

**Why this could fit for Maya:**
- Director title is the scope step-up Maya is targeting (one level up
  from Senior PM, with formal direct reports).
- Payments + SMB segment is adjacent to Maya's billing/fintech experience —
  different end user (SMB vs B2B SaaS company) but similar product surface.
- Small team means high autonomy and visibility.

**Risks / things to probe carefully:**
- **Stage maturity:** Series B with ~85 employees and pre-revenue is
  earlier than Maya's stated Tier 1 target. Higher equity upside but
  higher risk.
- **Comp:** Recruiter said "competitive for stage" — Series B startups
  often can't match $265K+ base. This may not clear Maya's floor.
  Resolve comp early in the screen.
- **Consumer-adjacent:** Maya's profile says "not interested in pure
  consumer fintech." Globex is SMB-focused, but the UX is consumer-grade.
  Maya should pressure-test whether this counts as consumer in spirit.
- **Reports-to:** Head of Product is brand new (Jan 2026). Same VP
  Product concern as Acme — Maya should test their PM philosophy.

**Questions Maya should ask:**
- "What's the comp band? I want to make sure we're aligned before
  going deeper."
- "How does the Director of Product role differ from Head of Product?
  What's the team structure 12 months out?"
- "You're calling this SMB, but the UX feels consumer-first. How do
  you think about that internally?"
